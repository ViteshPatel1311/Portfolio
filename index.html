Design & 3D Prototyping of Bionic Spacer Panel

An innovative approach to aircraft interior components using additive manufacturing.

Project Overview

This project focused on redesigning a standard aircraft spacer panel—a component used to fill gaps between overhead storage compartments—by leveraging the advantages of 3D printing. The primary goal was to significantly reduce the component's weight without compromising its structural integrity, thereby contributing to fuel efficiency and establishing additive manufacturing as a viable solution for the aviation industry.

Objectives

  • Reduce panel weight by at least 15% compared to conventional manufacturing.
  • Determine geometric characteristics and steady loads acting on the panel.
  • Utilize lattice structures to increase strength-to-weight ratio.
  • Create a flight-ready, aesthetically pleasing 3D-printed prototype.

Design & Prototyping Process

The design was modeled in CATIA V5, incorporating a bionic, lattice-based internal structure. This complex geometry, difficult to achieve with traditional methods, was optimized for strength and minimal material usage. The prototype was then printed using a CREALITY ENDER 3 PRO with PLA material. Post-processing ensured a smooth, high-quality finish suitable for an aircraft cabin interior.

Analysis & Results

Structural analysis was performed in CATIA's Generative Structural Analysis workbench. A force of 150 N was applied to simulate in-flight loads, with the results confirming the structural integrity of the new design. The final 3D-printed panel was **15% lighter** than its conventionally manufactured counterpart, successfully meeting the project's primary objective and demonstrating the enormous benefits of additive manufacturing in aerospace.

Project Details

  • Company: Ziegler Aerospace
  • Role: Design Intern
  • Tools: CATIA V5, CURA, 3D Printing (FDM)
  • Key Outcome: 15% weight reduction
